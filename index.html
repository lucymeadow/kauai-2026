Packing

Recommended Packing List

Beach & Water

  • Swimsuits (2–3 per person — one is always drying)
  • Rash guards / swim shirts (sun protection for the kids especially)
  • Snorkel masks (or buy/rent on island — Costco has cheap sets)
  • Water shoes / reef shoes (for Queen's Bath, Ke'e, rocky entries)
  • Beach towels (check if the house provides them — many VRBOs do)
  • Pop-up beach shade tent (for Owen)
  • Swim goggles for Will (and extras — they always get lost)
  • Pool toys — dive sticks, pool noodles, inflatable ball
  • Backup floatie / puddle jumper for Will (just in case — even though he's done with floaties!)
  • Sand toys for Will & Owen
  • Waterproof phone pouch

Sun Protection

  • Reef-safe sunscreen (required by Hawaii law — or buy at Costco Lihue)
  • Sun hats — wide brim for the adults, bucket hats for the kids
  • Sunglasses (+ strap for the little ones)
  • Aloe vera gel (for the inevitable first-day burn)
  • UV sun shirt / coverup for beach-to-lunch transitions

Clothing

  • Lightweight shorts, t-shirts, sundresses — keep it casual
  • One nicer outfit each for Bar Acuda farewell dinner
  • Light layers for evenings — it cools down after sunset
  • Light hoodie or windbreaker (for the Na Pali cruise — it gets breezy on the water)
  • Rain jacket or packable poncho (brief tropical showers happen)
  • Pajamas / loungewear for lanai evenings

Shoes

  • Flip flops / sandals (daily drivers)
  • Water shoes (rocky beaches & Queen's Bath)
  • Trail sneakers or hiking sandals (Waimea Canyon lookouts, Queen's Bath trail)
  • Golf shoes for Tom, Shmug & Matthew
  • One pair of casual nice shoes for dinner out
  • Note: the Na Pali cruise is barefoot — no shoes needed!

Kids — Will & Owen

  • Swim diapers (Owen)
  • Stroller (for Kilauea Lighthouse, shopping, luau)
  • Car seats (2 — one per car)
  • Portable sound machine / white noise for nap & bedtime
  • Favorite stuffed animal / blanket for sleep
  • Snacks for the car (lots of 1+ hr drives)
  • Sippy cups / water bottles
  • Baby carrier for Owen (beach access trails can be uneven)
  • Mess-proof bibs for restaurants
  • A couple of small toys / books for downtime

Golf — Tom, Shmug & Matthew

  • Golf clubs (or rent at the courses)
  • Golf shoes
  • Golf gloves
  • Golf shirts / shorts
  • Extra golf balls (the Prince Course is beautiful but punishing)
  • Hat / visor for the course

Biking

  • Bikes are at the property — just bring helmets if you want them (especially for Will)

Travel & Misc

  • Reusable water bottles (stay hydrated — it's humid)
  • Daypack / beach bag
  • Small cooler bag (for beach snacks & drinks from Costco)
  • Insect repellent (mosquitoes near rivers and at dusk)
  • First aid basics (band-aids, Benadryl, Dramamine for the cruise)
  • Chargers & portable battery pack
  • Camera / GoPro (the Na Pali Coast demands it)
  • Ziploc bags (phones on the boat, wet swimsuits)
  • Reusable shopping bags (Hawaii charges for bags)

Don't Bother Packing

  • Groceries / drinks — Costco Lihue on arrival
  • Snorkel gear — the house has sets, or buy cheap at Costco
  • Beach chairs — bulky; the house or beach parks have what you need
  • Fancy clothes — Kauai is extremely casual, even at nice restaurants
  • Umbrellas — rain is warm and brief; a light jacket is enough
  • Heavy jackets — lows are ~70°F in June
Practical

Practical Notes

Groceries & Supplies

  • Costco Lihue — Hit this on arrival day and potentially again mid-week. Best Costco you'll ever visit.
  • Princeville Shopping Center — Has a small grocery/general store for day-to-day needs.
  • Foodland in Princeville — Full grocery store if you need a mid-week restock.

Getting Around

  • Princeville to Hanalei: ~10 minutes
  • Princeville to Kilauea: ~15 minutes
  • Princeville to Lihue/Airport: ~45 minutes
  • Princeville to Waimea Canyon entrance: ~1.5 hours
  • Princeville to Port Allen (Na Pali cruises): ~1.5 hours
  • Princeville to Poipu (South Shore): ~1+ hours

Tips for Traveling with the Little Ones

  • Pack for the nap transition: Have a car seat setup where Owen can fall asleep on the drive home around noon.
  • Reef-safe sunscreen only — Hawaii law requires it. Buy at Costco on arrival.
  • Water shoes — The rocky shoreline at several beaches (especially Queen's Bath and Ke'e) is much easier with water shoes for little feet.
  • Beach shade — Bring or buy a pop-up beach tent for Owen. Available at Costco or ABC Stores.

For Tom & Susan

  • The itinerary builds in rest time every afternoon. The house is home base, and there's no pressure to do every activity.
  • Anini Beach and Pu'u Poa Beach are the most accessible, calmest options close to home.
  • The Princeville Shopping Center (B's Kitchen, Lappert's Ice Cream, The Spot) is a 3-minute drive for an easy outing.
  • Sunset walks to Punahele Point from the house are flat and short.

Weather in June

  • June is dry season on Kauai's North Shore — expect warm days (75–85°F), occasional brief rain showers, and spectacular sunsets.
  • Ocean conditions are generally calm in summer (unlike winter), making North Shore beaches much safer.
  • Queen's Bath is typically accessible in summer, but always check conditions and the gate.
